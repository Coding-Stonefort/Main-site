## How Does Stock CFD Trading Work with Stonefort?

Stock trading through CFDs allows you to speculate on the price movement of company shares without owning the underlying stock. Instead of purchasing physical shares, you trade contracts based on whether a stock’s price is expected to rise or fall, offering greater flexibility and efficiency.

One of the key advantages of trading stock CFDs with Stonefort is access to global equity markets from a single platform. With a relatively small initial deposit, traders can gain exposure to leading international stocks and take advantage of both upward and downward market movements.

Stonefort offers leverage of up to **1:20** on stock CFDs, enabling traders to increase their market exposure with lower capital outlay. For example, a **$1,000** margin can provide exposure equivalent to **$20,000** in stock value. While leverage can magnify potential returns, it also increases risk — making sound risk management and disciplined trading essential.

## Example of Stock CFD Trading

### How Is Profit Calculated?

**Opening Price**  
40 shares × $180 = **$7,200**

**Closing Price**  
40 shares × $195 = **$7,800**

**Total Profit from Trading**  
$7,800 − $7,200 = **$600**

---

### The Position Opened

40 CFDs of a stock were traded at an opening price of **$180** per share.  
The total value of the position is:

40 × $180 = **$7,200**

With a **10%** margin requirement, the required trading capital is:

10% of $7,200 = **$720**

This allows you to control a larger position with a smaller initial deposit.

---

### Position Closure

Two weeks later, the stock price rises to **$195** per share. You close your position at this higher price.

Total closing value:

40 × $195 = **$7,800**

Total profit realized from the price movement is:

**$600**
