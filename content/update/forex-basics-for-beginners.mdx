## What is Forex?

Forex, short for *foreign exchange*, is the global marketplace where currencies are traded.  
It’s the largest financial market in the world, with daily trading volumes exceeding **$7 trillion**.  
Every time you travel abroad and exchange your money for another currency, you are participating in the forex market  just on a much smaller scale.

Traders participate in forex to capture profit from the rise and fall of one currency against another.  
These movements may seem small, but with the right tools and understanding, they can create meaningful opportunities.

---

## Why Forex Attracts Newcomers

Forex has become one of the most accessible financial markets for beginners. Here's why:

### **High Liquidity**
The market sees massive buy and sell activity every second.  
This means traders can quickly enter and exit positions without large price slippage.

### **Market Hours**
Because forex operates across different time zones  from Sydney to Tokyo, London, and New York  the market runs **24 hours a day, 5 days a week**.  
You can trade at a time that fits your schedule.

### **Low Starting Capital**
Many brokers allow traders to start with small amounts, making forex more accessible than stocks or commodities.

### **Use of Leverage**
Leverage allows traders to control larger positions with smaller capital.  
However, with greater opportunity comes greater risk  understanding leverage is essential.

---

## Understanding Currency Pairs

Currencies are always traded in **pairs**, such as EUR/USD or GBP/JPY.  
The first currency is called the **base currency**, and the second is the **quote currency**.  
When you buy a pair, you’re buying the base currency and selling the quote currency.

**Example:**  
If EUR/USD rises, it means the euro is strengthening against the US dollar.

---

## Key Forex Terms Explained

To trade confidently, you’ll need to understand the basic terms used across the industry.

### **Pip**
A *pip* (Percentage in Point) is the smallest unit of change in a currency pair’s price.  
For most pairs, one pip equals **0.0001**.  
For JPY pairs, it’s **0.01**.

Pips help measure how much a trade moves and how much profit or loss it generates.

---

### **Lot**
A *lot* is the standardized trading size in forex.  
There are three main types:

- **Standard lot:** 100,000 units  
- **Mini lot:** 10,000 units  
- **Micro lot:** 1,000 units  

New traders often start with micro or mini lots to manage risk more effectively.

---

### **Spread**
The *spread* is the difference between the **bid price** (sell) and the **ask price** (buy).  
Spreads can be tight or wide depending on the market’s liquidity.  
Lower spreads generally benefit traders because they reduce trading costs.

---

### **Leverage**
Leverage lets traders control positions that are larger than their account balance.  
For example, with **1:100 leverage**, $100 allows you to control $10,000 worth of currency.

Leverage magnifies both earnings and losses  treat it with respect.

---

## Final Thoughts

Forex offers a world of opportunity, but success begins with a strong foundation.  
Understanding terms like pips, lots, spreads, and leverage will help you approach the market with clarity and confidence.  
As you continue learning, practice on a demo account, study price movements, and always manage your risk wisely.
